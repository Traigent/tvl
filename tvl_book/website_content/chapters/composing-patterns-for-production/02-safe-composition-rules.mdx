Composition rules deserve the same skepticism as overlays. A new bundle should not silently widen
risk just because it combines individually attractive components.

In practice, safe composition often means:

- fewer active controllers in production than in experimentation
- tighter retry or branch budgets in staging and rollout
- manifest evidence that records exactly which bundle shape was promoted

<ConceptCallout title="Carry Over the Overlay Mindset" tone="info">

Use the same instinct as <Term id="safe-narrowing">safe narrowing</Term>: when in doubt, make the
production composition more constrained and easier to audit than the exploratory version.

</ConceptCallout>

The right default is conservative. If a bundle increases uncertainty about who made the decision,
what evidence was used, or how to roll back, it is probably not ready for the production plane.

<PitfallCard title="Bundle Without Provenance">

The most dangerous multi-pattern systems are not always the most complex. They are the ones that
cannot explain, after the fact, which controller fired, which judge won, or which shard output
actually drove the release decision.

</PitfallCard>

<KnowledgeCheck prompt="How does safe narrowing apply to pattern composition?">

It means production bundles should generally be more constrained, more replayable, and easier to
audit than exploratory bundles unless there is strong evidence that a broader composition is still
safe and governable.

</KnowledgeCheck>
