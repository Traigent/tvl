Production systems rarely stop at one pattern. The real challenge is composition: which scaffold is
the base, which controller sits on top, and what evidence says the bundle is better than the simpler
alternative?

This chapter keeps composition conservative. The goal is not to create the most sophisticated agent.
The goal is to combine patterns only when the resulting control surface is still understandable,
measurable, and governable.

<ConceptCallout title="Bias Toward Simplicity" tone="tip">

The burden of proof is on the bundle, not on the simpler baseline. A pattern stack should earn its
complexity with measurable lift and a stable operational story.

</ConceptCallout>
