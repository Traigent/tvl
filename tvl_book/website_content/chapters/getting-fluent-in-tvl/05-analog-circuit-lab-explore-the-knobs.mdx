Try the Orientation RAG micro simulation to feel each tuning variable. The sliders map directly to the spec above;
latency, cost, and quality update in real time, and constraint warnings match the rules we enforce in Chapter 3.

<PredictionPrompt prompt="Before you touch the controls, predict which change will usually hurt latency fastest: broader retrieval, heavier reranking, or longer responses.">

In this lab, broader retrieval and longer responses tend to move latency first because they widen the amount of work
per request. Reranking matters too, but it is easier to understand once you see the latency and quality gauges move
together.

</PredictionPrompt>

**Suggested Prompts**

- Start from the **Baseline** preset, bump `retriever_top_k` to 56, and observe the latency gauge. When does the red
  guardrail trigger?
- Move the `rerank_weight` slider below 0.3 while `retriever_top_k` ≥ 40. What warning do you see? Why does the
  constraint exist?
- Hold `retriever_top_k` steady and stretch `response_tokens`. What trade-off emerges between cost and quality?

<KnowledgeCheck prompt="What should this lab teach you before Chapter 3 formalizes the rule system?">

It should make one idea concrete: TVARs are not abstract config fields. Each knob changes cost, latency, and quality,
and some combinations should be ruled out before the optimizer wastes trials on them.

</KnowledgeCheck>
