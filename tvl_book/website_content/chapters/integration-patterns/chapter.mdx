TVL earns its keep when paired with real engineering workflow: a validator in CI, an optimizer that
explores candidate configurations, and evidence artifacts that support rollout review.

In our stack, those surrounding systems include Triagent/TVO for optimization and DVL for dataset
validation. But the chapter is broader than those names. The core lesson is how a TVL contract fits
between local authoring, automated checks, experimentation, and promotion decisions.

<ConceptCallout title="How to Read This Chapter" tone="hint">

- First, learn the static toolchain and CI gates.
- Then connect TVL to runtime systems like TVO and DVL.
- Finally, treat manifests as the operational record that makes audits, rollback, and reproducibility possible.

</ConceptCallout>

<ConceptCallout title="Simple System Boundary" tone="integration">

- TVL declares the contract.
- CI and local tooling validate the contract before studies run.
- An optimizer consumes the contract and searches the approved surface.
- Promotion artifacts explain why one candidate is allowed to ship.

If you use our wider stack, <Term id="triagent-integration">Triagent/TVO</Term>, <Term id="dvl-integration">DVL</Term>,
and <Term id="opal-bridge">OPAL</Term> fill those downstream roles. The contract still stays in TVL.

</ConceptCallout>
