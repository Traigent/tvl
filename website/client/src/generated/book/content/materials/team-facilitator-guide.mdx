## Session Setup

- Ask learners to complete the **Quickstart** path or at least read Chapters 1 and 2.
- Send the Quickstart Lab Pack in advance.
- Prepare one domain-specific example from your own stack so the discussion is not purely abstract.
- Plan for 6-12 people; if the group is larger, use breakout groups of 3-5.
- Bring a shared doc or whiteboard so open questions and follow-up actions are captured live.

## Suggested Agenda

### Block 1. Why TVL Exists In Your Stack (10 min)

Facilitator action:

- explain one concrete under-governed decision from your own system
- show where TVL sits relative to the surrounding stack

Learner action:

- answer: which decisions in your system are currently under-governed?

Success signal:

- the room can name at least one decision that should be governed rather than left implicit

### Block 2. Validate One Example Together (15 min)

Facilitator action:

- project one small example and walk through it field by field
- introduce one deliberate mistake and ask the room what TVL would catch

Learner action:

- predict whether the mistake is structural, operational, or outside TVL

Success signal:

- learners can explain what TVL validates quickly and what still belongs in runtime or promotion evidence

### Block 3. Inspect One Constraint Or Overlay Scenario (20 min)

Facilitator action:

- split the room into groups
- give each group one constraint or overlay scenario

Learner action:

- decide whether the scenario belongs in the base contract, an overlay, or downstream tooling

Success signal:

- each group returns with one explicit judgment and one risk they would still want reviewed

### Block 4. Compare Promotion Evidence Expectations (20 min)

Facilitator action:

- rejoin the room and ask each group what evidence they would require before rollout

Learner action:

- answer: what would count as credible evidence before rollout in your environment?

Success signal:

- the room can distinguish a better demo from a promotable change

### Block 5. Capture Open Questions And Assign Next Material (10 min + 5 min buffer)

Facilitator action:

- write unresolved questions live
- assign the next material by learner role

Learner action:

- name which path or material they should take next and why

Success signal:

- every participant leaves with one concrete follow-up and the session ends with captured misconceptions rather than
  unspoken confusion

## Facilitator Notes

- If the room stalls in Block 1, ask for one recent incident or repeated tuning argument rather than a general opinion.
- If Block 2 becomes too tool-focused, bring the discussion back to what the error means for a reviewer.
- If Block 3 goes abstract, force the choice: contract, overlay, or downstream tooling.
- If Block 4 becomes debate without evidence, ask what artifact would let another engineer replay the decision later.

## Recommended Follow-Up

- New individual contributors: Quickstart Lab Pack
  Use this when the learner still needs one end-to-end pass from minimal spec to validation and microsim intuition.
- Technical leads: Architecture Review Workshop
  Use this when the learner is reviewing contracts, overlays, and evidence bundles across teams.
- Operators and SREs: Operator Rollout Drill
  Use this when the learner owns rollout safety, hotfix review, and rollback judgment.
- Mixed cohort finishing the book: Capstone Governed Adaptation Challenge
  Use this when the cohort is ready to defend one full governed design rather than discuss isolated concepts.
