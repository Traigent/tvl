This chapter is about ruling out bad experiments early.

In practice, most authors only need two ideas:

1. **Structural Rules** block bad combinations of design choices before exploration starts.
2. **Operational Preconditions** block a study when the current `env.context.*` conditions make it infeasible.

The solver details matter, but they are not the place to start. First learn the boundary between those
two rule types and what each one is supposed to protect.

<ConceptCallout title="Mental Model" tone="info">

Structural rules answer: "which combinations should never be tried?"

Operational preconditions answer: "is the current operating situation acceptable for running this study at all?"

</ConceptCallout>
