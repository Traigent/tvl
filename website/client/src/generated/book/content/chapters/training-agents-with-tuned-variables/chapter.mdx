Agent systems are often described with pattern names such as routing, debate, reflection, or map-reduce.
Those names are useful, but they are not the operational contract. What matters in practice is the
control surface underneath them: budgets, counts, ordering, judges, reducers, stop rules, and
evidence thresholds.

This chapter translates that idea into a TVL-oriented workflow. Instead of treating an agent as a
frozen prompt stack, treat it as a governed system with explicit tuned variables and measured release
criteria.

<ConceptCallout title="Simple Framing" tone="info">

- The pattern name tells you the scaffold.
- The tuned variables tell you what can change.
- The promotion policy tells you when a changed scaffold is safe enough to ship.

</ConceptCallout>

<ConceptCallout title="Why This Chapter Exists" tone="hint">

Many teams can sketch an agent architecture but still cannot answer the deployment question: which
parts are allowed to vary, how much experimentation budget is justified, and what evidence would make
an updated workflow promotable?

</ConceptCallout>
